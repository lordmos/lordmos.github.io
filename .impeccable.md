# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
**Primary**: Chinese-speaking developers and tech enthusiasts who discover LordMOS through GitHub, Zhihu, or Bilibili. They arrive to explore open-source Agent projects, read technical blog posts, or evaluate collaboration potential. They browse on both desktop (at work/home) and mobile (commuting, social media links).

**Secondary**: Potential users of iOS apps (唯沃壁纸, 微声白噪音, NFC 小册, 文小印) or the WeChat mini program (UA 真选组), arriving via App Store searches or word-of-mouth.

**Context**: Casual browsing — not urgent, not transactional. Users are exploring, getting a feel for who this person is, what they've built, and whether their work is interesting.

### Brand Personality
**Voice**: Casual, self-aware, unpretentious. The kind of developer who says "创业未半而中道崩崩崩崩 🫠" and calls themselves "AI Agent 菜鸟" — honest about the chaos of indie development, not performing expertise.

**3-word personality**: **Candid · Crafty · Grounded**

**Emotional goals**: Visitors should feel like they're meeting a real person — someone who builds interesting things, doesn't take themselves too seriously, and is genuinely enthusiastic about their craft. The vibe is "come see what I've been tinkering with" rather than "here is my impressive portfolio."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Tone**: Understated workshop — clean surfaces with personality showing through content, not decoration. The design should get out of the way and let the projects speak, but shouldn't be so minimal it feels impersonal.

**Theme**: Light by default, dark mode supported (prefers-color-scheme). The audience browses in mixed contexts — daytime work screens and nighttime phone scrolling.

**What this is NOT**:
- Not a corporate portfolio (no hero metrics, no client logos, no testimonial carousels)
- Not a "developer landing page template" (no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no neon accents)
- Not a design showcase (the owner is a developer, not a designer — the site should feel developer-authored, not designer-curated)

**References**: The current design is closest to a Japanese indie developer homepage — restrained, content-focused, with personality coming through writing rather than visual flourish.

### Design Principles

1. **Content is the design.** The projects, blog posts, and bio ARE the interesting parts. Visual design should frame them clearly, not compete with them.

2. **Personality through words, not pixels.** The emoji, the casual Chinese copy, the self-deprecating humor — these are the brand. Don't try to express personality through flashy visual effects; let the writing carry the character.

3. **Respectfully minimal.** Not minimal-for-the-sake-of-minimal (which reads as lazy), but minimal because every element earns its place. If something doesn't help the visitor understand who LordMOS is or find a project, it shouldn't be there.

4. **Developer-honest.** The site should feel like something a skilled developer would actually build for themselves — well-crafted code, clean structure, no unnecessary dependencies. The quality shows in the engineering, not in visual spectacle.

5. **Mobile-first in spirit.** Many visitors arrive from social links (Zhihu, Bilibili, WeChat). The mobile experience should feel native-quality, not a shrunken desktop page.

### Technical Constraints
- Pure static HTML/CSS/JS — no build step, no framework
- Hosted on GitHub Pages
- Chinese-language primary, some English section labels
- Must remain lightweight and fast-loading
- CSS variables for theming, `prefers-color-scheme` dark mode
